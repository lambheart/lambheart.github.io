Reading List

Last updated 2026 March 11

This is an actively maintained library of reading materials that I personally recommend to my friends and peers, either because they were profound and transformative, or good introductory texts to an array of subject matters. Sometimes, I list only a writer name.

The page serves as a bookmark for my friends in case they ever want to recall a recommendation I’ve made.

Each listing redirects to a Wikipedia page for the book, or the most all-encompassing resource after that.

FICTION

  • Ulysses, James Joyce
  • The Gadfly, Ethel Voynich
  • James Baldwin
  • 2666, Roberto Bolaño
  • Dream of the Red Chamber, Cao Xueqin
  • Oresteia, Aeschylus
  • Antigone, Jean Anouilh

POETRY

  • Prometheus Unbound, Percy Bysshe Shelley
  • Paradise Lost, John Milton
  • Autobiography of Red, Anne Carson
  • When I Say Love, Meredith Martinez

FILM

  • The Color of Pomegranates (Sayat Nova) (1969), dir. Sergei Parajanov

ESSAY/SPEECH

  • The Artist’s Struggle for Integrity (vid.), James Baldwin

CHINA

  • How China Works: An Introduction to China’s State-led Economic Development, Xiaohuan Lan
  • Party Life: Chinese Governance and the World Beyond Liberalism, Eric Li

SOCIOLOGY

  • Ruling Oneself Out, Ivan Ermakoff
